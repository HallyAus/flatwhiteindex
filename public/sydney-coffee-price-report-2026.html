Sydney Coffee Price Report · April 2026

The average coffee price in Sydney is $5.80

An independent survey of flat white prices across 23 Sydney suburbs, collected by direct phone call to 847 independent cafes. Updated monthly.

In one paragraph

A regular flat white in Sydney costs $5.80 on average in 2026, ranging from $4.00 in Ashfield to $7.20 at Darling Harbour.

Sydney's coffee prices vary by $3.20 between cheapest and dearest cafes — an 80% spread across a 30-kilometre radius. Inner-west suburbs (Ashfield, Marrickville, Strathfield) consistently price below $5.00. Eastern suburbs and harbour-adjacent areas (Mosman, Double Bay, Darling Harbour) consistently exceed $6.50. The most common single price across all 847 cafes surveyed is $5.50.

Frequently asked questions

The questions Sydney coffee drinkers (and journalists) ask us most.

What does a flat white cost in Sydney in 2026?
The average regular flat white in Sydney costs $5.80 as of April 2026, based on 847 direct cafe phone calls across 23 suburbs. Prices range from $4.00 (Cornerstone Espresso, Ashfield) to over $7.20 in Darling Harbour and Double Bay.
Where is the cheapest flat white in Sydney?
Ashfield has the cheapest average price at $4.60. The single cheapest cafe is Cornerstone Espresso in Ashfield at $4.00. Inner west suburbs — Marrickville ($4.80), Strathfield ($4.80) and Ashfield — consistently price below $5.00.
Where is the most expensive flat white in Sydney?
Darling Harbour and Double Bay tie at $7.20 average. Mosman ($7.00), Bondi ($6.90), Kirribilli ($6.80) and the CBD ($6.80) round out the top five.
How much should a flat white cost in Sydney?
A reasonable price for a regular flat white in Sydney is $5.00 to $6.00. Anything under $5.00 is cheap; over $7.00 is premium. The most common single price across all 847 cafes is $5.50.
Why are coffee prices in Sydney so different by suburb?
Three factors explain the $3.20 price gap: rent (harbour and eastern suburbs are expensive per square metre), foot-traffic mix (tourist areas command premium prices) and bean sourcing (specialty roasters charge more than wholesale). Inner west suburbs combine lower rent with strong specialty culture, producing the city's best value.
How do Sydney coffee prices compare to Melbourne?
Melbourne pricing is a separate survey launching soon. Anecdotally Melbourne CBD averages $4.80–$5.20 for a flat white — slightly cheaper than Sydney's $5.80 average. Get notified when the Melbourne survey launches.
How is this price data collected?
By direct phone call. An AI voice agent calls each cafe and asks one question — "How much is a regular flat white?" — then extracts the answer from the transcript. No menu scraping, no delivery-app aggregation, no user-submitted prices. Chain cafes are excluded. Read the full methodology.

Methodology in brief

The Flat White Index is the only Sydney coffee price survey collected by direct phone contact with each cafe. Every price you see comes from a real call, recorded and transcribed. We don't aggregate menus from delivery apps, scrape websites, or accept unverified user reports.

Coverage: 23 Sydney suburbs (Phase 1), 847 independent cafes called. Chain franchises (Starbucks, Gloria Jean's, etc.) are excluded — only independent venues are surveyed.

Update cadence: Prices are recalled monthly. The dataset is licensed CC BY 4.0 and free to use with attribution.
